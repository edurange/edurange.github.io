Annotated Bibliography - Information Security Resources

Sources Compiled by Carrie Rivers

  • Anderson, Ross. Security Engineering: A Guide to Building Dependable Distributed Systems. Indianapolis: Wiley, 2008. Print.

    • In this work, Ross Anderson provides an introduction to information security, social engineering, protocols, access controls, and cryptography. He discusses common challenges for distributed systems, and explores the concepts of Multilateral/Compartmentalization Security (MLS or MAC).
  • Ladkin, Peter. "The Risks Digest Volume 24: Issue 3." The Risks Digest. N.p., 31 Aug. 2005. Web. 26 June 2013. http://catless.ncl.ac.uk/Risks/24.03.html

    • Ladkin explains how the flaw in 777 systems that cause a loss of control for the pilots, possible catastrophic fault.
  • Schlichting, Richard D., and Fred B. Schneider. "Fail-Stop Processors: An Approach to Designing Fault-Tolerant Computing Systems." Cornell Edu. N.p., 1983. Web. 27 June 2013. http://www.cs.cornell.edu/fbs/publications/FailStop.pdf

    • This paper provides guidance for using fail-stop processors to make your system more fault tolerant. It also includes methods for mending some of the typical issues that arise from implementing this kind of system.
  • "University of Wisconsin - Madison and NETGEAR Joint Statement on NTP." University of Wisconsin - Madison and NETGEAR Joint Statement on NTP. N.p., n.d. Web. 27 June 2013. http://kb.netgear.com/app/answers/detail/a_id/1112/~/university-of-wisconsin-—-madison-and-netgear-joint-statement-on-ntp

    • This is a statement put out by Netgear that tactfully touches on a naming error they made so that routers pinged the UW-M to get the right time code. It also details the fix.
  • Clayton, Richard. "Light Blue Touchpaper." Light Blue Touchpaper RSS. N.p., 7 Apr. 2006. Web. 27 June 2013. http://www.lightbluetouchpaper.org/2006/04/07/when-firmware-attacks-ddos-by-d-link/

    • In the article, Clayton Richard analyzes D-Link’s naming error that flooded Poul-Honning’s servers with NTP requests.
  • Blass, Evan. "D-Link Relents, Agrees to Halt Its "NTP Vandalism"" Engadget. N.p., 11 May 2006. Web. 27 June 2013. http://www.engadget.com/2006/05/11/d-link-relents-agrees-to-halt-its-ntp-vandalism/

    • In this blog entry, Evan Blass highlights D-Link’s admission of naming error and their fix that included financial compensation and an update.
  • Shostack, Adam, and Andrew Stewart. The New School of Information Security. Upper Saddle River, NJ: Addison-Wesley, 2008. Print.

    • This book is a review of the current state of InfoSec and provides evidence-based opinions on where and how the field needs to evolve.
  • Anderson, Ross. "Why Information Security Is Hard: An Economic Perspective." University of Cambridge. Cambridge Computer Laboratory, n.d. Web. 07 July 2013. http://www.cl.cam.ac.uk/~rja14/Papers/econ.pdf

    • This is a summary of various impacts that economic realities have on the field of InfoSec.
  • Akerlof, George. "The Market for Lemons: Uncertainty and the Market Mechanism." The Quarterly Journal of Economics 84.3 (1970): 488-500. Print.

    • This is a journal article that details the “market for lemons” economic theory that deals with asymmetric information and its failings.
  • Schneier, Bruce. Secrets and Lies: Digital Security in a Networked World ; [with New Information about Post-9/11 Security]. Indianapolis, IN: Wiley, 2008. Print.

    • In this book Schneier tells the story of what digital security looks like, the setting and its flaws, the characters and their motivations, the tech and its limitations, the current security measures and their shortcomings, and finally he provides some best practices for developing excellent security.
  • Elliot. "Sniffing Keystrokes via Laser, Power lines." Hack a Day. N.p., 20 Mar. 2009. Web. 17 July 2013.

    • In this article, Elliot gives an example of work being done using power to “read” what a computer is doing.
  • Greene, Tim. "How to Use Electrical Outlets and Cheap Lasers to Steal Data." Network World. N.p., 09 July 2009. Web. 17 July 2013.

    • Here Tim Greene gives an example of ways people are stealing data via electric outlets.
  • Constantin, Lucian. "Chinese Malware Attack Affected Dozens of South Korean Organizations, Researchers Say." PCWorld. N.p., 26 Jan. 2013. Web. 18 July 2013.

    • This article details Chinese use of malware that subverted the CA and created a seemingly valid cert that allowed the malware through to steal data.
  • Scott, Andi. "How to Create a Good Information Security Policy." How to Create a Good Information Security Policy. N.p., n.d. Web. 22 July 2013.

    • This article provides a good starting place for developing a good InfoSec policy.
  • Bell, D. Elliot, and Leonard J. LaPadula. Secure Computer Systems: Mathematical Foundations. University at Albany - SUNY. University at Albany, Nov. 1996. Web. 23 July 2013.

    • This is an electronic version of the original 1973 paper. It details the security policy model designed by Bell and LaPadula.
  • Todd, Malcom, Adrian Brown, and Richard Blake. "Functional Requirements for the Sustainability of Electronic Records." http://www.nationalarchives.gov.uk/documents/functional_requirements.pdf The National Archive, 1 Mar. 2006. Web. 23 July 2013.

    • This provides more detail on the UK govt. standards around classifications.
  • "Standards and Requirements for Electronic Records Management." Standards and Requirements for Electronic Records Management. N.p., n.d. Web. 23 July 2013. http://www.nas.gov.uk/recordKeeping/ERGuidance/Standards.asp

    • This website details more UK standards or and provides a collection of links to various information handling standards.
  • "Electronic Information Management Standards." Electronic Information Management Standards. N.p., n.d. Web. 23 July 2013. http://www.archives.gov/records-mgmt/initiatives/standards.html

    • This is the US version of the website above. It is a portal that leads to the specific DOD guidelines of information management.
  • "Common Criteria." : New CC Portal., n.d. Web. 24 July 2013. www.commoncriteriaportal.org

    • This is a portal that leads to guidelines for developing MLS.
  • Rutkowska, Joanna. "The Invisible Things Lab's Blog." : Running Vista Every Day! N.p., 04 Feb. 2007. Web. 25 July 2013.

    • This blog entry covers MAC on Vista that translates to more modern Windows OS. It includes some interesting vulnerability info on virtualization and some flaws in the works.
  • Spencer, Ray, Stephen Smalley, Peter Loscocco, Mike Hibler, David Andersen, and Jay Lepreau. University of Utah ED. University of Utah, 1999. Web. 25 July 2013. http://www.cs.utah.edu/flux/papers/flask-usenixsec99.pdf

    • This is the paper that establishes the security framework used as a foundation for SELinux and Red Hat.
    "Trusted RUBIX." Trusted RUBIX. N.p., n.d. Web. 25 July 2013. http://rubix.com/cms/ This is a website that details RUBIX, a newer MAC.
